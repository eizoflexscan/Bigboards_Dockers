General idea

Global outlier approaches apply a model to represent normal data points â€“ Outliers are points that do not fit to that model. In the case of Univariate data analysis , it means that, given a certain kind of statistical distribution (e.g., Gaussian), outliers are points that have a low probability to be generated by the overall distribution (e.g., deviate more than 3 times the standard deviation from the mean).
Deviations from the mean are plotted in a control charts . Those charts derive from data a center line, an upper and a lower bound, highlighting as outliers all records out of those limits.





Assumptions

  • Normal data objects follow a (known) distribution and occur in a high probability region of this model.
  • Outliers deviate strongly from this distribution.

Model Limits

  • Basic model assumes only one normal generating mechanism , but there is usually more than one generating mechanism underlying the normal data.
  • Basic model assumes that outliers are rare observations, but there may be a large class of similar objects that are the outliers.
  • Outliers are also in the reference set and may falsify the results because mean and standard deviation are very sensitive to outliers.
